Jan. 17th: The End

Battle of the Fates

As the week draws to an end I am closer and closer to freedom. The grey wall has been removed now. I don't know how it was removed but I assume that the invisible men had something to do with it. As I get closer to freedom I am trying to remember what the outside world looks like. All I can remember is that it is cold and dark. I'm not sure what it was that I liked about the outside world before. I have everything that I need in here. If it was my choice I would stay but the other members of the club tell me I should go home. They keep using phrases like "Get some help Jacob.", "You really need to get outside.", and "Please leave the club room. You are creeping out our guests".


It seems then that the time has come for me to leave, but I cannot leave yet. The terrorist that has been causing havoc since the start must be taken down. I cannot allow him to continue terrorizing computer engineers. There is something that I must do for the sake of all present and future Computer Engineering Students. THE FILING CABINET MUST BE DEFEATED!!!!



My initial weapon of choice was a sword.



It appeared to be expecting this because I was quickly disarmed.



Next I grabbed a bow. Not my best weapon but still effective.



The cabinet was too quick for me and knocked the bow aside



Luckily I had a second sword ready



And I blocked the killing blow just in time.



The filing cabinet took the high ground.



It underestimated my powers.



I struck back



And wounded it.



It retreated back to its corner but not fast enough. I dealt it the killing blow before it could get away.



The computer engineering room is now safe and sound. Everyone rejoice in the defeat of the great and evil filing cabinet. COMPE! COMPE! COMPE! COMPE!

The Journey Home

I now realize that I did not choose this mission for myself. It was written in time before it even began. Even though I do not want to leave, I know that I must. My mission is over; I must go outside and face the cold harsh world. Goodbye ETLC. You still have many great mysteries that I have not yet discovered but it seems that fate has decreed for them to remain mysteries.
